Wind-Turbine Icing & Energy Forecasting

Python

98% icing classification accuracy
2.2–7.6 MWh estimated energy loss

Objective

Quantify icing-induced production losses and assess the long-term wind-energy potential of an alpine site.

Method

Combined SCADA data with heated and unheated anemometer measurements, labelled icing events, and trained classification and regression models.

Result

Identified 272 icing events. Estimated losses represented 5–18% of production, while long-term models indicated strong site potential.

My contribution

Data preparation, icing-event labelling, machine-learning modelling, statistical evaluation and energy-yield analysis.

CFD–Wind-Tunnel Correlation for a Foiling Catamaran

ANSYS Fluent · Python

±10% component-level pressure-loss agreement
51° oblique net incidence investigated

Objective

Investigate the discrepancy between previous wind-tunnel measurements and CFD drag predictions for a TF35 foiling catamaran.

Method

Reproduced reduced-scale wind-tunnel conditions and compared resolved-net and porous-media modelling strategies.

Result

Matched component-level pressure losses after correcting porous parameters, but showed that the approach could not fully reproduce the net behaviour on the complete boat.

My contribution

CFD setup, wind-tunnel correlation, porous-media assessment and interpretation of modelling limitations.

Slot-Width Effects on a Two-Element Wingsail

ANSYS Fluent · Python

Re ≈ 10⁷ fully turbulent regime
0.5–5% slot-width range

Objective

Determine when the slot between two wingsail elements improves aerodynamic performance.

Method

Ran a 2D steady-RANS parametric study across angle of attack, camber and slot width after mesh- and domain-convergence checks.

Result

No universal optimum was found. Wider gaps became more beneficial at high angles of attack, while performance depended strongly on camber and operating point.

My contribution

Geometry parameterisation, convergence studies, CFD campaign and aerodynamic post-processing.

Environmental CFD · Multiphase flow

Particle-Laden Thermally Buoyant Jets

ANSYS Fluent

5 × 10⁴ Lagrangian parcels
1–10 K temperature-difference range

Objective

Assess how thermal buoyancy changes a horizontal jet trajectory and the resulting particle deposition.

Method

Coupled a 3D steady-RANS carrier phase with a Lagrangian Discrete Phase Model, including mesh convergence and sediment trapping.

Result

Thermal buoyancy strongly deflected the plume, while deposition changed noticeably only for a limited fraction of particles.

My contribution

Simulation setup, mesh-convergence analysis, multiphase modelling and plume/deposition post-processing.

Formula Student CFD–Wind-Tunnel Correlation

3DEXPERIENCE · 3D printing · Python

<10% drag-coefficient error
149 wind-tunnel tests

Objective

Validate the team’s CFD methodology using a reduced-scale Formula Student race-car model.

Method

Designed and tested a 1:3-scale 3D-printed model, then compared measured aerodynamic coefficients and flow observations with CFD.

Result

Drag correlation was within 10%. Lift proved more sensitive to support effects, model deformation and measurement uncertainty.

My contribution

Reduced-model CAD, test planning, experimental campaign and CFD correlation.

Pneumatic-Tube Effects on Pressure Measurements

Python

36% fluctuation attenuation
13 tube lengths tested

Objective

Quantify how pneumatic-tube length affects steady and unsteady wall-pressure measurements.

Method

Designed a cylinder experiment, acquired pressure signals for several angular positions, and performed statistical and spectral analyses.

Result

Mean pressure coefficients remained nearly unchanged, whereas longer tubes attenuated pressure fluctuations and shifted spectral content.

My contribution

Experimental design, acquisition workflow, statistics, Fourier analysis and interpretation of the measurement chain.

Experimental mechanics · Fracture

Crack-Tip Fields from Digital Image Correlation

MATLAB · Ncorr

10 Hz image acquisition
20 µm/s crosshead speed

Objective

Assess whether linear-elastic fracture mechanics remains valid for highly deformable polyacrylamide hydrogels.

Method

Used Digital Image Correlation to obtain displacement and strain fields, calculated the J-integral and compared linear and nonlinear stress-intensity estimates.

Result

The linear model agreed well with two samples, while a third highlighted sensitivity to nonlinear material behaviour and experimental conditions.

My contribution

DIC processing, stress-field reconstruction, J-integral implementation and comparison of fracture models.

Biomechanics · Mechanical design · Impact testing

Biofidelic Neck Model for Ski-Helmet Testing

Autodesk Fusion 360 · Python

19.2 kHz sampling frequency
≈10 kN peak force with compliant neck

Objective

Introduce controlled neck-like compliance into a conventional rigid helmet-impact test setup.

Method

Designed a spring-based, manufacturable neck with guides and an end-stop, then compared rigid and compliant configurations in drop-tower tests.

Result

The compliant configuration reduced peak force from approximately 12–13 kN to about 10 kN and reduced exposure above the 250 g threshold.

My contribution

Concept selection, CAD design, component sizing, prototype integration and test-data analysis.

Lift and Drag of a Flapped 2D Airfoil

MATLAB

5 hinge positions
4 flap angles

Objective

Reproduce lift and pressure-distribution trends from a published two-dimensional airfoil study.

Method

Implemented Thin Airfoil Theory with an arbitrary flap-hinge location and compared multiple flap-angle and hinge-position configurations.

Result

The analytical model reproduced low-angle trends efficiently, while deviations increased near separation and stall where inviscid theory is limited.

My contribution

MATLAB implementation, geometry parameterisation and validation against reference results.

Finite elements · Structural mechanics

Structural Simulation of a Quadcopter Drone

Abaqus · Python · Excel

6.87 MPa maximum stress
0.17 mm maximum displacement

Objective

Validate an FPV drone frame under normal-flight loads and identify opportunities for weight reduction.

Method

Performed modal and static finite-element analyses with mesh-convergence studies for hovering, camera integration and asymmetric turning.

Result

The most demanding turning case remained far below the aluminium yield strength, supporting further mass optimisation.

My contribution

Load-case definition, FE modelling, convergence studies and structural-margin assessment.
